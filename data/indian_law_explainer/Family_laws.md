## 1. Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

### Objective
The **Hindu Marriage Act, 1955** codifies the law relating to **marriage, divorce, restitution of conjugal rights, judicial separation, nullity of marriage, and maintenance** among Hindus.

### Applicability
Applies to:
- Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs
- Persons who are not Muslims, Christians, Parsis, or Jews

### Key Definitions

- **Marriage**: A legally recognized union between two Hindus as per conditions of the Act.
- **Bride / Bridegroom**: Female and male parties to the marriage.
- **Sapinda Relationship**: Relationship by blood extending up to certain generations.

### Conditions for a Valid Hindu Marriage (Section 5)

- Neither party has a living spouse at the time of marriage
- Parties are capable of giving valid consent
- Parties are not within prohibited degrees of relationship
- Parties are not sapindas of each other (unless custom permits)

### Void and Voidable Marriages

- **Void Marriage**: Bigamous or within prohibited relationship.
- **Voidable Marriage**: Consent obtained by force or fraud, impotence.

### Matrimonial Reliefs

- **Restitution of Conjugal Rights**: Court may order cohabitation.
- **Judicial Separation**: Suspension of marital rights without divorce.
- **Divorce**: Grounds include cruelty, desertion, adultery, conversion, mental disorder.

### Maintenance and Alimony

- **Permanent Alimony**: Financial support after divorce.
- **Maintenance pendente lite**: Maintenance during proceedings.

## 2. Hindu Succession Act, 1956

### Objective
The **Hindu Succession Act, 1956** governs **intestate succession** (when a person dies without a will) among Hindus.

### Key Definitions

- **Intestate**: Person dying without a valid will.
- **Heir**: Person entitled to inherit property.
- **Coparcenary**: Joint family property under Hindu law.

### Classes of Heirs

- **Class I Heirs**: Son, daughter, widow, mother (equal rights).
- **Class II Heirs**: Father, siblings, etc. (inherit if no Class I heirs).

### Rights of Women

- Daughters are **coparceners by birth** (after 2005 amendment).
- Absolute ownership of property inherited by a female Hindu.

### Succession Rules

- Property devolves first to Class I heirs
- Equal distribution among heirs of same class

## 3. Special Marriage Act, 1954

### Objective
The **Special Marriage Act, 1954** provides a **civil form of marriage** irrespective of religion, caste, or faith.

### Nature of Marriage

- Secular and non‚Äëreligious
- Registration-based marriage

### Conditions for Marriage

- Monogamy
- Soundness of mind
- Minimum age (21 for male, 18 for female)
- Not within prohibited relationships

### Marriage Procedure

- Notice of intended marriage
- Objections (if any)
- Solemnization and registration

### Matrimonial Reliefs

- Divorce, judicial separation, nullity
- Grounds similar to Hindu Marriage Act

### Succession

- Succession governed by **Indian Succession Act, 1925** unless parties opt otherwise

## 4. Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937

### Objective
Ensures application of **Muslim Personal Law (Shariat)** to Muslims in matters of personal status.

### Scope
Applies to matters relating to:

- Marriage (Nikah)
- Divorce (Talaq)
- Maintenance
- Dower (Mehr)
- Guardianship
- Succession and inheritance

### Key Concepts

- **Nikah**: Civil contract of marriage
- **Mehr (Dower)**: Sum payable by husband to wife
- **Talaq**: Dissolution of marriage by husband (subject to judicial limits)

### Nature of Law

- Based on religious principles
- Interpreted through courts and constitutional values
